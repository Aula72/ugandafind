Economy of Uganda

Uganda is endowed with significant natural resources including ample fertile land, regular rainfall and mineral deposits. It’s thought that Uganda could feed the whole of Africa if were commercially farmed. The bank of Uganda is a symbol of Uganda’s economy and was established in 1966 by the Act of Parliament

Uganda’s Agriculture

Uganda’s key agricultural products can be divided in to cash crops, food crops and horticultural produce. The most important cash crops are coffee, tea, cotton, tobacco and cocoa.

National Emblem

The crested crane is Uganda’s national emblem. The grey crowned crane was chosen as Uganda’s national emblem because of its beauty and elegance. It’s a bird of national significance occupying the prime position on the country’s national flag and the coat of arms. The crested crane has been Uganda’s symbol for nearly 100 years

Culture in Uganda

Uganda has a very strong cultural heritage with many regions having kingdoms including Buganda, Busoga, Bunyoro and Toro. Ugandans are remarkably hospitable and hail from a diversity of rich cultures and life styles. Each tribe has its own traditional dance.

Education in Uganda

Makerere University Kampala is the epitome of Uganda’s education success. The university is Uganda’s largest and the third oldest institution of learning. First established as a technical school in 1922, it became the university of East Africa in 1963, offering courses leading to general degrees from the University of London

Geography of Uganda

Uganda is a landlocked country in East Africa whose diverse landscape encompasses the snow-capped Ruwenzori Mountains and the immense Lake Victoria. Its abundant wildlife includes chimpanzees, wild animals and rare birds.

Religion in Uganda

Uganda’s religious heritage is tripartite; that is Christianity, Islam and the African Traditional Religion. About four fifth of the population is Christian primarily divided between Roman Catholic and the Anglican Church. Mostly Anglicans but also including Pentecostals, seventh day Adventists, Baptists.
